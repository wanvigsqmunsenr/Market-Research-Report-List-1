<p><p>Contact level sensors are classified into several market types based on their sensing technology. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves to detect liquid levels, while microwave/radar sensors utilize electromagnetic waves for non-contact measurement. Optical sensors employ light refraction or reflection for detecting levels, and laser sensors provide precise distance measurement using focused light beams. Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems (MEMS) offer miniaturized sensing solutions with high sensitivity. Other markets may include pressure, capacitive, and float-based sensors, each tailored for specific applications and environments.</p></p>
